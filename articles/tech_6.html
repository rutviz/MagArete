Visual Prosthetics Restored Sight

Weaponized appendages. Weaponized appendages. Wi-Fi- connected eyeballs. Real mind control. Medical advancements like these were formerly the wheelhouse of science fiction. But in the next few years, the promise of 3-D printing, brain prosthetics and bionic body parts will yield a number of drastic improvements in science and medicine. Researchers believe that by 2027, the human body could look drastically different.

Similarly, natural calamities aren’t something we mere humans can control and it goes without saying it’s never a good sight after a natural calamity hits a place lots of destruction, death and injured.

We can stop natural calamities and it is going to be quite long before we turn into a Utopian World without wars so some people spend millions to come up with various medicines and prosthetic limbs and not just that a new kind of breakthrough has led to the invention of visual prosthetics.

While the more futuristic research of repairing and upgrading eyesight is already in development, visual prosthetics company Second Sight has created the Argus II, a visual prosthetic that sits on top of the retina. Using an antenna that sends signals back and forth between a special pair of glasses, the Argus II can bypass damaged parts of the eye and send visual signals straight to the brain — meaning it doesn't just bring vision from poor to fair, it could restore sight completely.


With this new invention people with less or no eyesight do not have to rely on people to donate their eyes for them to be able to see again they can see and enjoy the colourful world around them.
