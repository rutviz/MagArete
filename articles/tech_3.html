EVOLUTION IN CHARGING OF CELL PHONES

Hello friends, Today I am going to explain the different charging techniques. After reading this article you will be able to understand different charging techniques and also you will know some new and amazing things related to upcoming charging techniques.

Cell phone was firstly invented in 1973 by Martin Cooper. World’s first cell phone was launched by the company Motorola DynaTAC , which gives you the 30 minutes talk time and this mobile was able to stand 6 hours on standby mode, but to be fully charged ,it takes 10 hours. As the time moved on and the technology was modified the Nokia 3310,Nokia n70,Sony Ericson w800i this phones came into existence. This phones took much less time to charge itself as compared to Motorola because these phones batteries did not have much power storage and also these phones did not contain many features...

In the hustle and bustle modern life, a smartphone is the most important device. Smartphone plays a vital role in today's life. But in today's smartphone , we can consider the battery life as its weakest point. The average smartphone does not remain charged for longer time. Now the way to improve the battery life is to stuff a bigger battery have more capacity. However that’s not the exact solution because for elegant look smartphone companies make a slim and compact smartphone. But for this problem, the solution is FASTCHARGING,TURBOCHARGING ,DASHCHARGING and other charging techniques. Even though the name might be different but the underlined technology is essentially the same. All the above charging methods works on the principle of Applying additional current at asset battery voltage results in more power ,which can result in fast charging but also require sturdier charging components that can withstand high power level.

The different thing is that Have you ever thought about generating electricity from plants using the leaves as a solar panel??? This is the first green energy generator “BIOO LITE”

This system collects the electricity in the daytime as well as at night.As any other mobile charging system,you have to connect the USB cable to make the system work.Basically, this is a simple pot in which you can keep any plant.This system works on the principle of photosynthesis.In photosynthesis, the plants use the sunlight to synthesise nutrients from carbon dioxide and water.After photosynthesis the plants releases too many biological elements .At the bottom of the pot,there is an anode a cathode and too many microorganisms,many active biomolecules,biological nanowires etc...It will attract the biological elements which have been released by the plants,and after attraction some reactions takes place and it will generate the electrons.After the generation of electrons,electrons make a kind of bridge from cathode to anode through nanowires which are connected to the USB port which is placed at the top of the pot.The USB port gives you the output of 5V 1 amp.And this is one-time investment.....

What about future??? In future, we are able to fully charge our phone only in 5 seconds. This thing is possible because of newly discovered material GRAPHENE. It is one form of carbon. By the use of this material SUPERCAPACITOR is made. The Indian origin girl EESHA KHARE is presently working on the project of super capacitor. Khare's invention won $50,000 in prize money at the Intel Foundation Young Scientist Award held in Phoenix, Arizona. If we mix the fast charging property of capacitor and slow discharging property of battery then we get the super capacitor. Yet today the fabrication of Graphene on a mass scale is not possible because mass production needs to be economically viable. You can produce small quantities in Lab, it will cost around ten thousand dollars. It will not be viable to produce it on the large scale. There needs a method to produce it in a cheaper way...
