Programming Language : The Communication between living and non-living things

“परिवर्तन ही संसार का नियम हे।”

It is been said in the holy book “Bhagvad Gita” the evolution is the rule of world. It seems really true at every moment of our life, the things that we had just seen a minute before gets changed just in a minute, when we talk about the large time intervals its unpredictable but necessary too.

When it comes to the communication between the non-living things and living things, in the early centuries the communication were taking place with the mechanical devices through the physical movements, like communication between crib and his axe, if the crib wants to communicate with axe he has to move his hand so that the axe work accordingly.

As the time passes these communication techniques got changed from physical movements to logical plans, and these changes took place according to the need.

Programming languages have existed for over 200 years, since the invention of the punch-card- programmable Jacquard loom. It wasn’t a programming language in the modern sense as there was no computation and no logic but it started a cascade that would eventually lead to Charles Babbage’s Analytical Engine, and Ada Lovelace’s 1842 deconstruction of his work which led to the first computer program.

It was a whole 100 years before the first electrical, programmable computers would came into existence, however Machine specific assembly language in the 1940s was probably the first human readable programming language, but by the 1950s computer engineers realized that assembly language was taking too much time and effort and error-prone to build entire systems out of it. Thus in 1955 the first modern programming language was born: FORTRAN (FORmula TRANslator). LISP (LISt Processor), ALGOL (ALGOrithmic Language), and COBOL (COmmon Business Oriented Language) would follow in the next few years. Almost every language today is a derived from one of these first four languages FORTRAN, LISP, ALGOL and COBOL are still actively used by large, lumbering institutions.

Then the evolution of the programming languages got vast evolution speed, followed by languages like C(1969), PASCAL(1970), C++(1983), PERL(1987), PYTHON(1991), RUBY(1993), PHP(1995), JAVA(1995), JAVASCRIPT(1995), RUBY ON RAILS(2005) and the list is yet continue. The future is of Artificial intelligence languages.


Though the evolution of the programming languages looks exiting, there are always pros and cons behind every change, programming language evolution too have many. In early times it needs more physical and logical work to program something, and now physical work seems to disappear, and in future the logical work might also get disappear. But the benefits that we got are more important than it, the most important thing that matters is the time, the evolution brought a large change in time, in early times it was taking a days for a single computation, but nowadays thousands of computations can be performed in a second, and the future is unpredictable.
